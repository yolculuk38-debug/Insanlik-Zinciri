# HC:// Protocol

## What is HC://?

HC:// is an experimental open verification and digital fingerprint protocol designed for the AI era.

The goal of the protocol is simple:

Digital content should have a traceable identity layer.

---

## Why Does It Exist?

Modern digital systems face increasing problems related to:

- deepfakes
- synthetic media
- manipulated content
- disinformation
- unverifiable archives
- anonymous redistribution
- AI-generated content confusion

Humanity Chain attempts to create a transparent verification layer for digital content.

---

## Core Idea

HC:// works like a digital fingerprint system.

Each registered content item may receive:

- SHA-256 hash
- timestamp
- verification metadata
- optional QR reference
- optional witness validation

If the content changes, its fingerprint changes.

---

## Protocol Logic

HC:// is not designed to control the internet.

It is designed to create verifiable reference layers.

The protocol focuses on:

- traceability
- transparency
- integrity verification
- optional witness review
- open standards

---

## Comparison

### SSL
SSL helps verify website connections.

### HC://
HC:// helps verify digital content integrity.

---

## Verification Layers

Humanity Chain may support multiple layers:

### Base Layer
Basic fingerprint registration.

### Integrity Layer
Hash consistency verification.

### Witness Layer
Human or AI review systems.

### Multi-Witness Layer
Independent verification from multiple entities.

---

## AI Witness System

The AI witness layer is optional.

It may include:

- AI-assisted review
- manipulation analysis
- synthetic media indicators
- multi-model comparison
- verification notes

AI output should not be treated as absolute truth.

---

## Platform Integration

HC:// is designed as an open protocol layer.

Potential integrations may include:

- social platforms
- archives
- media systems
- news systems
- public records
- creator platforms
- educational systems

---

## Content Responsibility

HC:// does not assume ownership of uploaded content.

Responsibility remains with:

- uploader
- publisher
- platform
- content owner

The protocol only provides verification infrastructure.

---

## Open Standard Vision

Humanity Chain aims to support an open and transparent verification ecosystem.

The long-term vision is to create a globally understandable verification language for digital content.
